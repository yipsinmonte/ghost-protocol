Step 1 Recovery wallets ● Required

Recovery wallets are your safety net. If you ever lose access to this wallet, a recovery wallet can rescue your assets — without one, your assets could be permanently locked in the vault.

!
Add at least one recovery wallet
Without a recovery wallet, if you lose your seed phrase or hardware wallet, no one — including you — can move the assets out of your Ghost vault. They will be locked forever.

Edge cases this wireframe covers

  • Stuck-asset prevention. The Save Ghost button is disabled until at least one recovery wallet is added. Empty-state warning explains why in plain English.
  • NFT display name. Each beneficiary form has a "Display name" field that gets baked into the NFT image at mint time. Defaults to empty; placeholder suggests "Mom, Dad, Aunt Sue, your full name…".
  • Beneficiary removed after NFT minted (open question). If the owner later removes a beneficiary, their NFT becomes a stale promise. Current plan: the owner triggers a metadata update that flips attributes.Status from Active to Revoked, repoints image to a struck-through "REVOKED" variant, and the claim page checks on-chain before letting the holder act. Captured in TODO.md.
  • NFT copy is claim-ticket framing, not "you got it now." Pulse pill reads "Held in trust"; body explicitly says "You can claim them only if {{RELATION}} stops checking in, or names you as a recovery wallet." No instant-payout implication.
  • Post-deposit "no beneficiaries" banner. Not in this wireframe — separate flow that fires after the vault first holds assets and beneficiary_count == 0 AND whole_vault_recipient is None.
